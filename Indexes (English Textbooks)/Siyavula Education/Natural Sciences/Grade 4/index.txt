TABLE OF CONTENTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TERM 1

  Strand: Life and Living
    Topic: Living and non-living things
      - What is living and what is non-living?
      - Characteristics of living things (movement, growth, reproduction, feeding, breathing, sensitivity, excretion)
      - Sorting things into living and non-living
      - Things that were once living

    Topic: Structures of plants
      - Roots, stems, leaves and flowers
      - Functions of roots (anchor plant, absorb water and minerals)
      - Functions of stems (support, transport water and food)
      - Functions of leaves (make food using sunlight)
      - Functions of flowers (reproduction)
      - Different types of plants and their structures

    Topic: Structures of animals
      - Body structures of animals (head, body, limbs)
      - Structures for breathing (lungs, gills)
      - Structures for moving (legs, wings, fins, tails)
      - Structures for eating (teeth, beaks, tongues)
      - How body structures help animals to survive

TERM 2

  Strand: Life and Living
    Topic: What plants need to grow
      - Plants need water, sunlight, air and soil
      - Investigating what happens when plants do not get water
      - Investigating what happens when plants do not get sunlight
      - Investigating what happens when plants do not get air
      - Growing plants in different types of soil
      - Recording and comparing results

    Topic: Habitats of animals
      - What is a habitat?
      - Different types of habitats (forest, grassland, wetland, desert, ocean)
      - How animals are suited to their habitats
      - Animals in their local environment
      - How habitats can change

  Strand: Matter and Materials
    Topic: Materials around us
      - Natural materials (wood, stone, cotton, wool, sand, water)
      - Manufactured materials (plastic, glass, metal, paper, brick, cement)
      - Properties of materials (hard/soft, rough/smooth, waterproof/absorbent, strong/weak, flexible/stiff, transparent/opaque)
      - Choosing materials for different purposes

TERM 3

  Strand: Matter and Materials
    Topic: Solid materials
      - Properties of solid materials
      - Strong and weak materials
      - Stiff and flexible materials
      - Waterproof and absorbent materials
      - Testing and comparing solid materials
      - Choosing the right material for a purpose

    Topic: Strengthening materials
      - Why materials need to be strengthened
      - Ways of strengthening materials (folding, rolling, twisting)
      - Strengthening paper by folding and rolling
      - Investigating the strength of different shapes (tubes, triangles)

  Strand: Energy and Change
    Topic: Energy and energy transfer
      - What is energy?
      - Different forms of energy (light, heat, sound, movement)
      - Sources of energy (sun, food, fuels, electricity, batteries)
      - Energy transfer from one form to another
      - Energy in our daily lives

    Topic: Energy and sound
      - What is sound?
      - How sound is made (vibrations)
      - How sound travels
      - Loud and soft sounds
      - High and low sounds (pitch)
      - Making musical instruments

TERM 4

  Strand: Energy and Change
    Topic: Energy and light
      - Sources of light (natural and artificial)
      - Light travels in straight lines
      - Transparent, translucent and opaque materials
      - Shadows (how they are formed, changing the size and shape of shadows)
      - Reflection of light (mirrors, shiny surfaces)

  Strand: Earth and Beyond
    Topic: Planet Earth
      - The shape of the Earth (sphere)
      - Land and water on Earth
      - Rocks and soil on Earth
      - Features of the Earth's surface (mountains, valleys, rivers, oceans)
      - The Earth's resources (water, air, soil, minerals)
      - Looking after the Earth

    Topic: Rocks and soil
      - Different types of rocks
      - Properties of rocks (hardness, colour, texture, layers)
      - Comparing rocks
      - What soil is made of (small rock particles, humus, water, air)
      - Different types of soil (sandy, clay, loam)
      - Comparing soils (water holding, texture, particle size)
      - Uses of soil
