TERM 1

  Strand: Life and Living
    Topic: Photosynthesis
      - What is photosynthesis?
      - What plants need for photosynthesis (sunlight, water, carbon dioxide)
      - What plants produce during photosynthesis (glucose, oxygen)
      - Why photosynthesis is important for life on Earth
      - The role of chlorophyll and chloroplasts
      - Investigating photosynthesis (testing a leaf for starch)

    Topic: Nutrients in food
      - Why we need food
      - The main nutrients (carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, water, fibre)
      - Functions of each nutrient in the body
      - Sources of nutrients (foods that contain each nutrient)
      - A balanced diet
      - The South African food-based dietary guidelines
      - Reading food labels
      - Diseases caused by nutrient deficiency (kwashiorkor, marasmus, scurvy, rickets)

    Topic: Food processing
      - Why food is processed
      - Methods of food processing (drying, smoking, salting, canning, freezing, pasteurisation)
      - Advantages and disadvantages of food processing
      - Food additives (preservatives, colourants, flavourants)
      - Food safety and hygiene

TERM 2

  Strand: Life and Living
    Topic: Ecosystems
      - What is an ecosystem?
      - Components of an ecosystem (living and non-living)
      - Different types of ecosystems (grassland, forest, wetland, river, ocean, desert)
      - Relationships in ecosystems (feeding relationships, competition, symbiosis)
      - Food webs
      - Energy flow in ecosystems
      - How humans affect ecosystems (pollution, deforestation, overfishing)
      - Conservation and sustainability

  Strand: Matter and Materials
    Topic: Solids, liquids and gases
      - Three states of matter
      - Properties of solids (fixed shape, fixed volume)
      - Properties of liquids (no fixed shape, fixed volume)
      - Properties of gases (no fixed shape, no fixed volume)
      - Comparing solids, liquids and gases
      - Particle model of matter (how particles are arranged in each state)

    Topic: Changes of state
      - Melting and freezing (melting point, freezing point)
      - Evaporation and condensation (boiling point)
      - Sublimation
      - The effect of heating and cooling on matter
      - The water cycle (evaporation, condensation, precipitation)
      - Investigating changes of state

  Strand: Matter and Materials
    Topic: Mixtures
      - What is a mixture?
      - Types of mixtures (solutions, suspensions, alloys)
      - Dissolving (solute, solvent, solution)
      - Factors that affect dissolving (temperature, stirring, particle size)
      - Saturated solutions
      - Separating mixtures (sieving, filtering, evaporation, distillation, magnetism, decanting, hand sorting)
      - Investigating separation methods

  Strand: Energy and Change
    Topic: Electric circuits
      - What is an electric circuit?
      - Components of a circuit (cell/battery, wires, switch, bulb)
      - Drawing circuit diagrams (symbols)
      - Series circuits
      - Parallel circuits
      - Conductors and insulators in circuits
      - Building and testing circuits
      - The effect of adding more cells or bulbs

    Topic: Systems to solve problems
      - What is a system?
      - Input, process and output
      - Electrical systems in everyday life
      - Designing and making a system to solve a problem

TERM 4

  Strand: Energy and Change
    Topic: Energy and the Sun
      - The Sun as the main source of energy on Earth
      - How energy from the Sun reaches the Earth
      - The Sun and photosynthesis
      - The Sun and the water cycle
      - The Sun and weather
      - Solar energy (solar panels, solar water heaters)
      - Fossil fuels and the Sun (how coal, oil and gas formed from ancient living things)

  Strand: Earth and Beyond
    Topic: The solar system
      - What is the solar system?
      - The Sun (a star at the centre)
      - The eight planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune)
      - Characteristics of each planet (size, distance from Sun, surface features)
      - The Moon (Earth's natural satellite)
      - Other objects in the solar system (asteroids, comets, dwarf planets)
      - Day and night (rotation of the Earth)
      - Seasons (revolution of the Earth around the Sun, tilt of the Earth's axis)

    Topic: The Earth and the Moon
      - The rotation of the Earth (day and night, 24 hours)
      - The revolution of the Earth around the Sun (a year, 365¼ days)
      - Phases of the Moon (new moon, crescent, quarter, gibbous, full moon)
      - The Moon's revolution around the Earth (approximately 28 days)
      - Eclipses (solar eclipse, lunar eclipse)
