--- TABLE OF CONTENTS ---

Term 1
  Topic 1: The Atmosphere
    - Composition and structure of the atmosphere
    - Heating of the atmosphere (insolation, reflection, conduction, convection, greenhouse effect, global warming)
    - Moisture in the atmosphere (evaporation, condensation, humidity, cloud formation, precipitation types)
    - Reading and interpreting synoptic weather maps (station models, weather elements)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Using atlases
    - Fieldwork: weather instruments and data collection
    - GIS: satellite images related to meteorology

Term 2
  Topic 2: Geomorphology
    - The structure of the Earth (internal structure, rock classification, rock cycle, intrusive igneous features)
    - Plate tectonics (continental drift evidence, plate movement mechanics, processes at plate boundaries)
    - Folding and faulting (rock folding, landforms, types of faults, rift valleys, block mountains)
    - Earthquakes (occurrence, measurement, impact on people, strategies to reduce impact)
    - Volcanoes (types, structure, impact on people and environment)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Topographic maps: 1:50 000, conventional signs, compass directions, bearing
    - Aerial photographs and orthophoto maps
    - Mapwork: locating position, scale

Term 3
  Topic 3: Population
    - Population distribution and density (world patterns, factors affecting distribution)
    - Population structure (indicators: birth rates, death rates, life expectancy, population pyramids)
    - Population growth (demographic transition model, overpopulation)
    - Population movements (migration types, causes and effects, refugees, migrant labour)
    - HIV and AIDS (infection rates in southern Africa, social and economic effects)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Atlas skills: interpreting graphs, population pyramids, photographs
    - GIS: satellite images related to population

Term 4
  Topic 4: Water Resources
    - Water in the world (hydrological cycle, salt water and fresh water)
    - The world's oceans (ocean circulation, warm and cold currents, pollution, overfishing)
    - Water management in South Africa (rivers, dams, inter-basin transfers, water purification)
    - Floods (causes, flood hydrographs, management strategies, case study)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Topographic maps: landforms and contours
    - Aerial photographs and orthophoto maps
    - GIS: spatial objects, lines, points, nodes and scales
