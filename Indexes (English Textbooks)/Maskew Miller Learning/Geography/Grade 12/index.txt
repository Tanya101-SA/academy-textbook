--- TABLE OF CONTENTS ---

Term 1
  Topic 1: Climate and Weather
    - Mid-latitude cyclones (characteristics, formation, stages, weather patterns, fronts)
    - Tropical cyclones (formation, stages, satellite images, case study, impact, management)
    - Subtropical anticyclones (high-pressure cells, air circulation, travelling disturbances, berg winds)
    - Valley climates (micro-climate, anabatic and katabatic winds, inversions, frost pockets)
    - Urban climates (urban heat islands, pollution domes, strategies to reduce effects)
  Topic 2: Geomorphology
    - Drainage systems in South Africa (drainage basin, river types, drainage patterns, stream order)
    - Fluvial processes (river profiles, meanders, oxbow lakes, floodplains, waterfalls, rejuvenation, river capture)
    - Catchment and river management (importance, human impact, SA case study)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Synoptic weather maps and satellite images
    - Topographic maps: contours, cross-sections, gradient, intervisibility
    - GIS: catchment areas, paper GIS

Term 2
  Topic 3: Rural and Urban Settlement
    - Study of settlements (concept, site and situation, classification)
    - Rural settlements (location, classification by pattern and function, settlement shapes, land use)
    - Rural settlement issues (rural-urban migration, depopulation, land reform)
    - Urban settlements (origin, development, urbanisation, classification by function)
    - Urban hierarchies (central place, threshold population, sphere of influence)
    - Urban structure and patterns (land use zones, models: multiple-nuclei, South African city)
    - Urban settlement issues (planning, housing, overcrowding, informal settlements)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Mapwork skills: scale, contours, cross-sections
    - GIS: remote sensing, resolution, data types, data manipulation

Term 3
  Topic 4: Economic Geography of South Africa
    - Structure of the economy (primary, secondary, tertiary, quaternary sectors)
    - Agriculture (contribution to economy, small-scale and large-scale, food security)
    - Mining (significance, factors, case study of a main mineral)
    - Secondary and tertiary sectors (industry types, factors, industrial regions)
    - Strategies for industrial development (IDZs, SDIs, centralisation and decentralisation)
    - Informal sector (characteristics, challenges, case studies)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Topographic maps: scale, contours, grid referencing
    - GIS: satellite images, data analysis
    - Using atlases: thematic maps

Term 4
  Revision
    - Climate and weather
    - Geomorphology
    - Settlement geography (rural and urban)
    - Economic geography of South Africa
    - Geographical skills and techniques
