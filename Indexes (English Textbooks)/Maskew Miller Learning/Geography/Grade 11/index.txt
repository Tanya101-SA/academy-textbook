--- TABLE OF CONTENTS ---

Term 1
  Topic 1: The Atmosphere
    - The Earth's energy balance (unequal heating, significance of axis and revolution)
    - Global air circulation (pressure belts, tri-cellular circulation, Coriolis force, wind systems)
    - Africa's weather and climate (climate regions, El Nino and La Nina, synoptic weather maps)
    - Drought and desertification (areas at risk, causes, effects, management strategies)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Aerial photographs and orthophoto maps
    - GIS: satellite images, application to climatology

Term 2
  Topic 2: Geomorphology
    - Topography associated with horizontally layered rocks (plateaus, canyons, mesa, butte, scarp retreat)
    - Topography associated with inclined/tilted rock strata (cuesta, homoclinal ridge, hogsback)
    - Topography associated with massive igneous rocks (batholiths, granite domes and tors)
    - Slopes (types, slope elements: crest, cliff, talus, pediment, slope development and retreat)
    - Mass movements and human responses (soil creep, landslides, rock falls, mud flows, strategies)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Topographic maps: contours, cross-sections, vertical exaggeration, gradient
    - GIS: spatially referenced data, raster and vector data
    - Fieldwork: observation, collecting and recording data

Term 3
  Topic 3: Development Geography
    - The concept of development (terminology, indicators: GDP, HDI, Gini coefficient, life expectancy)
    - Frameworks for development (Rostow's model, core-periphery, sustainability, community-based)
    - Trade and development (international trade, free trade, globalisation, export-led development)
    - Development issues and challenges (role of women, environmental effects, role of state)
    - Role of development aid (types: technical, conditional, humanitarian, case studies)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Mapwork skills: topographic maps, direction, scale, distance, area
    - Using atlases: index, locating places, comparing maps

Term 4
  Topic 4: Resources and Sustainability
    - Using resources (relationship to economic development, exploitation, sustainability)
    - Soil and soil erosion (soil formation, causes, evidence in SA, management strategies)
    - Conventional energy sources (thermal, hydro, nuclear energy in SA, environmental impact)
    - Non-conventional energy sources (solar, wind energy, future potential in SA)
    - Energy management in South Africa (changing energy needs, greener economies)
  Geographical Skills and Techniques
    - Topographic maps revision: contours, cross-sections, intervisibility
    - GIS: spatially referenced data, data types
