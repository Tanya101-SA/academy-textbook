TABLE OF CONTENTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TERM 1
    Topic 1: Listening and Speaking
        1.1 Listening comprehension (identifying main and supporting ideas, speaker's tone and purpose)
        1.2 Prepared speech/oral presentation (research, structure, delivery, visual aids)
        1.3 Unprepared speech (impromptu response, coherent argument under time pressure)
        1.4 Group discussion (leadership, facilitation, constructive disagreement)
        1.5 Conversational skills (formal vs informal register, cultural sensitivity)

    Topic 2: Reading and Viewing
        2.1 Intensive reading and comprehension
            2.1.1 Critical reading strategies (questioning the text, evaluating credibility)
            2.1.2 Identifying purpose, audience, and context
            2.1.3 Inference and interpretation at deeper levels
            2.1.4 Distinguishing between explicit and implicit meaning
        2.2 Novel study
            2.2.1 Setting, plot structure, and narrative techniques
            2.2.2 Characterisation (direct, indirect, development, motivation)
            2.2.3 Themes, motifs, and symbols
            2.2.4 Narrator reliability and point of view (first person, third person, omniscient)
            2.2.5 Social, historical, and cultural context of the novel
        2.3 Poetry
            2.3.1 Detailed analysis of prescribed poems
            2.3.2 Poetic devices (imagery, symbolism, sound devices)
            2.3.3 Structure and form (sonnet, free verse, ballad, ode, elegy)
            2.3.4 Tone, mood, diction, and register in poetry
            2.3.5 Comparing and contrasting poems
        2.4 Visual literacy
            2.4.1 Analysing cartoons (caricature, speech bubbles, visual metaphor)
            2.4.2 Analysing photographs (composition, perspective, caption)
        2.5 Summary writing
            2.5.1 Summarising from multiple sources
            2.5.2 Distinguishing between essential and non-essential information
            2.5.3 Paraphrasing complex ideas concisely

    Topic 3: Writing and Presenting
        3.1 Essay writing
            3.1.1 Narrative essay (complex plot, flashback, multiple perspectives)
            3.1.2 Descriptive essay (advanced sensory detail, extended metaphor, atmosphere)
            3.1.3 Reflective essay (personal experience, critical reflection, insight)
        3.2 Transactional writing
            3.2.1 Friendly/informal letter (nuanced tone, varied purpose)
            3.2.2 Diary entry (extended personal reflection, emotive language)
            3.2.3 Dialogue (realistic speech patterns, subtext, character voice)
        3.3 Process writing
            3.3.1 Planning and mind-mapping
            3.3.2 Drafting with audience awareness
            3.3.3 Peer review and self-editing
            3.3.4 Proofreading for accuracy and style

    Topic 4: Language Structures and Conventions
        4.1 Parts of speech (advanced application)
            4.1.1 Nouns (gerunds, nominalisation)
            4.1.2 Pronouns (relative pronouns in complex sentences)
            4.1.3 Verbs (tense consistency, modal verbs, subjunctive mood)
            4.1.4 Adjectives and adverbs (effective word choice for impact)
            4.1.5 Prepositions and prepositional phrases
            4.1.6 Conjunctions (correlative conjunctions: both...and, neither...nor)
        4.2 Sentence structure (advanced)
            4.2.1 Simple, compound, and complex sentences (revision)
            4.2.2 Compound-complex sentences
            4.2.3 Sentence variety for stylistic effect
            4.2.4 Correct word order and syntax
        4.3 Punctuation (advanced)
            4.3.1 Semicolons and colons in complex writing
            4.3.2 Dashes and parentheses
            4.3.3 Ellipsis
            4.3.4 Punctuation in dialogue and quotations
        4.4 Vocabulary and word choice
            4.4.1 Etymology (word origins, roots, Greek and Latin prefixes/suffixes)
            4.4.2 Denotation and connotation (nuanced understanding)
            4.4.3 Idiomatic expressions and proverbs
        4.5 Register, tone, and diction (formal, informal, colloquial, slang)

    Formal Assessment: Oral (prepared speech), Comprehension test, Essay, Transactional writing

            8.5.3 Collocations and word families
