--- TABLE OF CONTENTS ---

Term 1
  Listening and Speaking
    - Group discussion: brainstorming, selecting relevant ideas, sequencing main ideas
    - Listening comprehension
    - Identify persuasive and manipulative techniques
  Reading and Viewing
    - Literary text: youth short stories
    - Key features: character, characterisation, plot, conflict, background, setting, narrator, theme
    - Reading process: pre-reading, during reading, post-reading
    - Parts of a book: title page, table of contents, chapters, glossary, index
  Writing and Presenting
    - Narrative/reflective essay
    - Word choice, personal voice and style, vivid description, tone
    - Process writing: planning, drafting, revision, editing, proof-reading
  Language Structures and Conventions
    - Word level: adverbs of place and degree, pronouns (personal, relative, reflexive, possessive)
    - Sentence level: subject and predicate, subject-verb agreement, phrases and clauses, parts of speech
    - Word meaning: synonyms, antonyms, literal and figurative meaning
    - Punctuation: full stop, comma, question mark, quotation marks, exclamation mark

Term 2
  Listening and Speaking
    - Listening comprehension: instructions and directions
    - Unprepared news presentation/speech
    - Giving directions: features, language and conventions, body language
  Reading and Viewing
    - Information text with visuals: maps, landmarks, scales
    - Poetry: internal and external structure, figures of speech, imagery, rhyme, rhythm
    - Typography, figurative meaning, mood, theme and message
    - Reading/viewing visual text: scanning, intensive reading, inferences, summaries
  Writing and Presenting
    - Transactional text: directions/instructions
    - Correct format, main and supporting ideas, paragraph conventions
    - Process writing: planning, drafting, revision, editing, proof-reading
  Language Structures and Conventions
    - Word level: adjectives (comparative, superlative), common and proper nouns, conjunctions
    - Sentence level: topic sentence, supporting sentences, tenses, simple and compound sentences
    - Word meaning: synonyms, antonyms, contextual meaning
    - Punctuation and spelling patterns

Term 3
  Listening and Speaking
    - Listening comprehension: recorded dialogue
    - Language and power, tone, mood
    - Group discussion/dialogue: roles, turn-taking, viewpoints, consensus
  Reading and Viewing
    - Literary text: drama/play
    - Key features: character, action, dialogue, plot, conflict, setting, theme
    - Poetry: figures of speech, imagery, rhyme, rhythm, stanzas
    - Reading strategies for comprehension: purpose, inferences, fact vs opinion
  Writing and Presenting
    - Longer transactional text: dialogue/review
    - Paragraph conventions, logical order, conjunctions for cohesion
    - Process writing: planning, drafting, revision, editing, proof-reading
  Language Structures and Conventions
    - Word level: adjectives (comparative, superlative), common and proper nouns
    - Sentence level: simple present tense, question forms, direct and reported speech, simple past tense
    - Word meaning: antonyms, synonyms, paronyms, contextual and literal meaning
    - Punctuation and spelling: quotation marks, spelling patterns

Term 4
  Listening and Speaking
    - Listening comprehension: written/audio-visual text
    - Identify persuasive/manipulating techniques
    - Conversation: discussion based on newspaper/magazine article
  Reading and Viewing
    - Newspaper/magazine article: format, language use, text features, sequencing
    - Poetry: internal and external structure, figures of speech, imagery
    - Reading/viewing for information: intensive reading, inferences, fact and opinion
  Writing and Presenting
    - Summary writing
    - Long transactional text: newspaper article/magazine article
    - Correct format, main and supporting ideas, cohesion
    - Process writing: planning, drafting, revision, editing, proof-reading
  Language Structures and Conventions
    - Word level: singular and plural, gender, diminutives
    - Sentence level: direct and reported speech, question forms, sentence structure, voice, gerund
    - Word meaning: synonyms, antonyms, euphemism, homonyms, literal and figurative meaning
    - Punctuation and spelling: quotation marks, spelling patterns
